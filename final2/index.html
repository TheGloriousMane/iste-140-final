The Finger Lakes

Learn More!

The Finger Lakes region of New York State is a hidden gem, offering a blend of natural beauty, rich history, and vibrant culture.

The region is named after its 11 long, narrow lakes, with Cayuga and Seneca being the largest. These lakes provide opportunities for boating, fishing, kayaking, and swimming. State parks like Watkins Glen, with its stunning gorge and waterfalls, and Letchworth, known as the "Grand Canyon of the East," are must-visit destinations.

The Finger Lakes is renowned for its wineries, with over 100 wineries offering tastings and tours, and the region is especially famous for its Riesling wines. Visitors can enjoy farm-to-table dining experiences, local cheeses, and the famous Finger Lakes grape pie. Exploring the charming towns like Skaneateles, Aurora, and Hammondsport, each with its unique history and attractions, adds to the appeal. The region boasts numerous museums, including the Glenn H. Curtiss Museum in Hammondsport.

Whether you're an outdoor enthusiast, a wine lover, or a history buff, the Finger Lakes Region has something for everyone. Plan your visit today and discover the beauty and charm of this enchanting area!
